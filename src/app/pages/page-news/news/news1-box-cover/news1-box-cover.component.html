Game box illustration

Written by Kristine Eide, 29/09/2019

Game box illustration


Choosing the right artist to create the best illustration for the game box is really important and something we have spent a lot of time on. We decided to go with the talented Tomas Duchek.

We wanted the style to remind you somewhat of the Norwegian national romantics of the 1800s with reference to Hans Gude, one of NorwayÂ´s foremost landscape painters from the 19th century. We had a really good process with Tomas where we sent him sketches along with reference photos of Norwegian fjords, stave churches and other medieval buildings. We also wanted the box cover to evoke associations to an album cover for a Norwegian metal band.

It was important for us that Fjordar should not look like a Viking game, since the period of the Norwegian civil war (1130 to 1240) is roughly 100 years after the viking age ended.

We got the art from Tomas this week and we have already gotten some really good feedback on it on social media along with useful input on how to make the logo easier to read. We look forward to working more with Tomas later when the rules of the game have been finally decided upon and he can start to create the illustrations for the character cards.
Last week we ordered boxes with the new illustration from Boardgamemakers and we are so excited to get our hands on them and to show them off on the upcoming RegnCon and SPIEL 2019!
